1. Mobile Computing

Mobile Computing refers to performing computation on portable devices such as smartphones, tablets, and embedded IoT devices. It is shaped by three major characteristics:

  • Mobility: Devices frequently change physical location and transition between Wi‚ÄëFi, 5G, and offline states.
  • Resource Constraints: Mobile devices operate on limited CPU, RAM, storage, and battery budgets.
  • Context Awareness: Devices leverage sensors (GPS, accelerometer, gyroscope, magnetometer, light sensors) to adapt behavior to user context.

Understanding these characteristics is essential for designing adaptive and energy-efficient applications.
